The Mayan Abacus is based on the Vigesimal or Base-20 system. Lets take a look at how mayan #’s are written. You could think of their numbers as being written in an imaginary box, separated by an imaginary line. You’d start at the bottom section. 1-4 would be represented by dots. A five would mean replacing the dots with a line. To go from 6-9 you’d draw a dot on top of that 5. And ten would mean another line above the previous. The pattern goes until you get to 20. Now you’d draw a shell replacing all the other dots. This represents 0, and a dot would be drawn in that imaginary top box. With these two boxes numbers up to 419 could be written. Then you’d add an extra box at the top and keep going. The Mayans used the Base 20 number system in conjunction with a Base 5 system. This may seem odd, until you learn that the mayans counted in twenties because a human has 20 digits(both hands and feet).
